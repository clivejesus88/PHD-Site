Founding Context

A Vision Born From Conviction

PHD emerged from a profound realization among ten founding members that youth potential was abundant but structurally underutilized. The founders identified a critical gap between spiritual calling, talent, and practical societal impact among young people.

This wasn't conceived as another conventional NGO, but as a Kingdom-centered movement—combining spiritual depth, institutional discipline, youth-led solutions, and long-term generational impact.

Revolutionary Approach

Young people positioned not as beneficiaries, but as "Solution Givers" to society's challenges.

Founding Pillars

Origin-Defining Resolutions

Humble Beginnings Mandate

Adopted the name Pull Heaven Down (PHD) Family Network International with a strategic posture recognizing that lasting institutional greatness is built through discipline, patience, and consistency.

Equality among members was affirmed, rejecting hierarchy based on status or background.

Kingdom Alignment

Resolved not to function as a secular NGO. Prayer, spiritual discernment, and alignment with God's purpose established as prerequisites for all projects and partnerships.

Faith-driven rather than donor-driven approach from inception.

Institutional Rigor

Explicitly rejected vision without structure. Committed to developing strategic roadmap, division of responsibilities, and personal ownership of mission by every member.

Institution-minded rather than informal identity.

Family Culture & Unity

Adopted covenant of togetherness and like-mindedness. Mentorship embedded as sustainability mechanism. Deliberately framed as family, not merely a network.

Youth-led rather than youth-targeted approach.
